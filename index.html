Municipality of Tudela

1. Location and Geography:

Tudela is located on the northern coast of Misamis Occidental and is part of the Zamboanga Peninsula region. It is bordered by the municipalities of Tangub City to the east, Plaridel to the south, and Oroquieta City to the west.

The town has a coastal geography, with access to Tangub Bay, making it an important area for both agriculture and fishing. The natural environment includes rolling hills, forests, and scenic coastlines.

2. Agriculture:

Tudela is primarily an agricultural town, with farming being the main source of livelihood for many of its residents. The fertile land is ideal for growing crops like rice, corn, coconut, bananas, and other fruits and vegetables.

The town is also known for its fisheries due to its coastal location, contributing to the local economy through the production of fish and seafood.

3. History:

Tudela was originally part of the municipality of Plaridel, which is now a neighboring town in Misamis Occidental. In the early 1900s, Tudela became a separate municipality.

The town was named after Tudela, a town in the Navarre region of northern Spain, likely due to Spanish influence during the colonial period.

4. Population and Culture:

Tudela has a relatively small population compared to other municipalities in Misamis Occidental. The population is around 40,000 to 50,000 people, with the majority being of Cebuano or Bisaya descent.

The town celebrates its annual fiesta in honor of San Isidro Labrador, the patron saint of farmers, including religious processions, cultural events, and various activities.

5. Economy:

Agriculture is the backbone of Tudela's economy, particularly coconut and corn production. Small-scale industries, food processing, handicrafts, and traditional arts are also present.

The town's proximity to Oroquieta City allows it to benefit from trade and commerce in the region.

6. Natural Attractions:

Tudela is known for its natural beauty, including beaches, caves, and waterfalls. Attractions include Tudela Bay, which provides opportunities for fishing and boating, and scenic views of the coastline.

Forests and rolling hills are home to various flora and fauna, making it appealing to nature lovers.

7. Tourism Potential:

While Tudela is not as developed in terms of tourism infrastructure, its natural attractions and agricultural charm are gaining attention. Eco-tourism is a growing sector, particularly with its quiet and peaceful environment.

Tudela Bay is ideal for eco-tourism activities such as boating, fishing, and nature tours, with potential for beach resorts and agri-tourism ventures.

8. Tudela Municipal Hall:

The Tudela Municipal Hall is the administrative center of the town, housing local government offices and community development initiatives.

9. Transportation:

Tudela is accessible via land transportation, with bus routes connecting it to nearby cities like Oroquieta City, Tangub City, and Dipolog City. Public transport is provided by tricycles and jeepneys.

10. Educational Institutions:

The town has public and private schools, including Tudela National High School, which offers secondary and vocational education programs.

11. Notable Landmarks:

Tudela Church, dedicated to San Isidro Labrador, is a historical and cultural landmark, serving as the center for religious and community activities, especially during the fiesta celebrations.

Local landmarks, such as parks and community centers, reflect Tudela's rural charm.

12. Environmental Initiatives:

Tudela has focused on environmental protection and sustainability, with initiatives to preserve natural landscapes, maintain clean beaches, and protect local flora and fauna.
